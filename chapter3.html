Chapter 3: The Numbers Game

Scene 1: The Discovery

Lincoln sat at his desk, surrounded by Elias Holden’s scattered notes. Most pages were filled with linguistic studies of ancient texts, but one stood out—handwritten numbers, repeated multiple times.

His eyes traced the sequence: 4:7:5, 19:3:2, 12:9:4, 7:14:6. Each set of numbers was written with near-obsessive precision.

“Holden wasn’t just collecting numbers… he was pointing at something,” Lincoln muttered.

At the top of the page, in faded ink, a phrase was scribbled: “The key is always within the Word.”

He typed the numbers into his computer, searching for patterns. Nothing obvious emerged. Then it clicked—these numbers might correspond to a **Bible verse**.

Scene 2: Seeking Advice

Lincoln hesitated but eventually emailed Dr. Prescott Chroix. Hours later, they sat across from each other in Chroix’s dimly lit office.

Lincoln explained what he had found, sliding Holden’s notes across the desk.

Chroix studied them, his fingers tapping rhythmically. “If Holden was encoding something within scripture, he wasn’t the first.”

He leaned back and rattled off examples—Talmudic scholars, medieval monks, even World War II spies who hid messages in religious texts.

Lincoln nodded but felt a growing unease—Chroix wasn’t just intrigued. He was invested.

“So,” Lincoln asked, “what would you do next?”

Chroix smirked. “You already know the answer, Lincoln.”

Scene 3: Decoding the Message

Back home, Lincoln cross-referenced the numbers with biblical texts. Each set pointed to a specific letter within a verse.

The sequence: **4:7:5, 19:3:2, 12:9:4, 7:14:6**

Using the **Book-Chapter-Verse-Letter** method, Lincoln extracted each letter, slowly assembling a phrase.
