Chapter 5: The Map’s Secret

A Narrow Escape

Lincoln barely had time to process the decoded message before Dorne’s smug expression darkened. The moment Lincoln scribbled down the translation, Dorne knew he was beaten.

“Give me the page,” Dorne said, stepping forward.

Lincoln tightened his grip on his notebook. “Not a chance.”

The monastery’s library was no place for a fight, but Lincoln had no doubt that Dorne wasn’t above making things physical. The man had resources, connections—Chroix’s influence backing him. But Lincoln had something else: a head start.

Without another word, he bolted.

The librarian, startled by the sudden movement, called out in protest, but Lincoln was already weaving through the stone corridors. Footsteps pounded behind him—Dorne was fast. Too fast.

Bursting through an arched doorway, Lincoln ducked into a side passage, pressing himself against the cold stone wall. Dorne’s steps thundered past. Lincoln exhaled, gripping his notebook. The map was hidden in plain sight. But where?

Following the Clue

Stepping cautiously into the monastery’s courtyard, Lincoln flipped back through his notes. The decoded message repeated in his mind:

“The key to the map is hidden in plain sight.”

His fingers traced the worn edges of the manuscript page he had taken. It wasn’t just an ancient text—it was a guide. The monks who had preserved it had left something behind. But what?

In the center of the courtyard stood a massive stone fountain. Its design was simple—too simple. But the base of the fountain bore intricate carvings, winding symbols that Lincoln hadn’t noticed before.

Then he saw it—a **worn engraving beneath the water’s edge**, only visible when the light struck it at a certain angle.
